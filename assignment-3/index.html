Breaded Chicken Strips

"Chicken Strips are the world's best food. Because I said so!"
-Jude MacDonald, 2018
Disclaimer: The image above the quote is not the picture for the product. It's a different recipe's picture. So it's likely to be different.

Ingredients

  • Eight White Bread Slices
  • 3 Tbls Grated Parmesan
  • 2 Tbls Minced Fresh Parsley Leaves
  • 3/4 tbs Kosher Salt
  • Freshly Ground Black Pepper
  • About 1 Cup All-Purpose Flour, for Dredging
  • 2 Large Eggs, Beaten
  • 3 Boneless Skinless Chicken Breasts, 8 oz. each
  • Vegetable Oil, for Shallow Frying

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 Degrees Fahrenheit
  2. To prepare bread crumbs: Tear the bread slices into pieces. Stick them in a microwave oven for one minute on High. After that, pulse it in a food processor with parmesan, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Put flour, beaten eggs, and bread crumbs into three seperate bowls.
  4. Pound the three chicken breasts to equal thickness in between two sheets of plastic wrap. Afterwards, cut into 1 1/2 inch strips and dry the strips. Dredge with flour, shake off any excess, lightly coat them in egg, let any excess drip back into egg bowl, finally coating them with bread crumbs and laying them onto a plate with wax paper lined on it.
  5. Heat 1/4 inch of oil in a hot skillet over medium heat until shimmering hot. Lay strips into the oil, don't turn them until the breading is golden brown --around two minutes in--. Turn the chicken, cook the other side until chicken is cooked through and golden brown. Rinse and repeat until done. Transfer cooked chicken onto a plate lined with paper towels. Serve and enjoy!
